Thanks for stopping by:

Can you double-check the model number of your set? It is definately a GJ2 chassis.... but I'm not getting anything in the database for 32f734 - I have a 32F704. Maybe yours is not a US model??

Here is a generic diagram that will explain what each component looks like....



Here is a Typical High voltage circuit in a CRT TV....



High on the "check" list is the snubber capacitor. This is AKA a safety capacitor.

 

Sometimes it may also open, short, or change its capacitance value - and this always has an effect on the H.O.T.

 

Next are dry solder joints around the horizontal drive and output components, please do some re-soldering around these components including the fly back pins and Horizontal drive transformer pins.

 

The drive transistor should be replaced directly without even measuring it, sometimes it develops some leakage and causes the H.O.T to short after a period of time anyway.

 

Also be sure to check the ESR of the filter capacitors involved in filtering the supply to horizontal circuits and if you suspect any replace that capacitor.

 

In some televisions like the JVC - they incorporate some component on path to the base of the HOT (usually a Capacitor and choke) please be sure to replace them as well.

 

If the HOT is still getting shorted then finally you may have to change the fly back transformer or the horizontal oscillator IC is messed up and needs replacement.

 

 

As for a service manual - there are plenty of copies of the AV-32F704 floating around the net... and it should give you a good idea of what you're looking at in the set, and be very close schematic wise. It is also a GJ2 chasss set.

 

Testing components in-circuit can only be done with an o-scope, and it may be worth a $40 visit to your local repair shop to have it troubleshot.... even if you want to do the work yourself after you get the diagnostic estimate. Most component level repairs can be done for not much more than that, though... and probably worth NOT having a headache over .

 

Good luck!!

Expert:  bluextc89 replied 2 years ago.
When you get sound - but no pic in a CRT... the biggest culprit is the vertical deflection circuit.

Vertical circuits can be tricky to repair. Without the proper test equipment, manials, and parts, repair can be very difficult. There are also components in the power supply that could affect vertical deflection.

Look closely at that vertical deflection IC - it's the IC mounted on that heatsink.
The vertical IC is usually a 7-9 pin IC mounted on a heat sink. You might flip the board over and see if their are any markings that say vertical or deflection circuit. Look for charring or bubbling of the plastic.

Strangely unable to find a manual or schematic... online or in my personal collection of paper manuals.

It likely pre-dates the internet as we know it, and a digital copy does not exist.
